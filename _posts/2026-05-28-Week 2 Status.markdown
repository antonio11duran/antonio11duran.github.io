This post is a part of a series of weekly status updates for an Android app project I am doing as a part of the Professional Development in Computer Science class at the University of Colorado Boulder.

## What did you do last week?

Last week (and a bit of this week), I created this GitHub pages using Jekyll. It was a bit challenging, in a good way, trying to follow the GitHub documentation and dealing with the version compatability issues when trying to install Ruby and Bundler. So far, I am happy with the result.

## What do you plan to do this week?

Since my project involves doing something I have never attempted before: creating an Android app using Kotlin and Android Studio. I am going through the Android developer site and completing the training courses Professor Guinn recommended via Piazza. I am currently going through the first recommendation and building the simple Android app to get my feet wet.

## Are there any impediments in your way?

I need to make sure I do not get too invested in the training courses. They are in depth and detailed, but I run the risk of trying to complete everything the course has to offer before jumping into my project. I need to ignore the completionist voice in my head and just use the training courses as a quick way to learn the neccessary tools to get started, then use my project as my main tool for learning.

## Reflection on the process you used last week, how can you make the process work better?

I definitely need to be better in making dedicated time in doing something project related everyday. My strategy last week was to do less frequent, but longer sessions. This was good in being able to feel productive, but is not sustainable due to life and work popping up. There is also the issue of hopping back into my work takes significantly more time as I need to remember what I was doing and where I left off. Shorter work sessions that occur more often will have the con of feeling less productive, but I will be able to hop in and out of things quicker.

A good strategy to implement is better note-taking. I can make an effort of keeping track of what I have accomplished day-to-day and be able to see where I left off. I think I will create a Trello board or a Google Keep checklist of tasks/timeline.
